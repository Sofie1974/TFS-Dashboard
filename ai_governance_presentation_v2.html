The Politics of AI

How Leadership Changes Shaped Global AI Governance (2020-2025)

The Global Shift

From High-Level Principles to Concrete Global Laws in 5 Years

From "Readiness" to "Implementation"

2020: The "Readiness" Era

Focus was on high-level ethical principles and assessing national "readiness."

Leadership was concentrated in North America and Western Europe.

Most "strategies" were non-binding documents.

2025: The "Implementation" Era

Focus has shifted to binding legal frameworks, like the EU AI Act, and enforcement.

Global participation from Africa, Latin America, and Asia.

Rise of dedicated AI Safety Institutes to test new models.

The Great Divergence

The biggest factor in policy change was not technology, but politics.

The Core Thesis

Political stability was the single biggest factor in allowing a country to accelerate its AI governance. Political change forced a re-evaluation, pivot, or a complete reversal.

Case Study: The Two Extremes

United States (Reversal)

2020-2024: Focus on safeguards, culminating in the "Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy AI" Executive Order.

2025: New administration revokes the order, framing safety rules as "barriers to progress."

China (Acceleration)

2020-2024: Implemented some of the world's first binding rules for generative AI, prioritizing state control.

2025: Launched "AI Plus" initiative to embed AI in the economy and export its governance model.
